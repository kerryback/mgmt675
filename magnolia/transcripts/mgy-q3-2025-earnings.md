# Magnolia Oil and Gas (MGY) Q3 2025 Earnings Call Transcript
Source: Motley Fool, October 30, 2025

## Key Speakers
- Christopher Stavros - Chief Executive Officer
- Brian Corales - Chief Financial Officer

## Executive Summary
Magnolia Oil & Gas delivered strong Q3 2025 results with record production of 100,500 BOE/day, representing 11% year-over-year growth. The company generated $219 million in adjusted EBITDAX with a 31% operating margin while maintaining disciplined capital spending at 54% of EBITDAX.

## Financial Highlights
- Record quarterly production: 100,500 BOE/day (Q3 2025)
- Free cash flow: $134 million
- Adjusted EBITDAX: $219 million
- Cash balance: $280 million (highest in 2025)
- Total liquidity: ~$730 million
- Capital reinvestment rate: 54% of adjusted EBITDAX
- Shareholder returns: $80 million (60% of free cash flow)
- Share repurchases: 2.1+ million shares
- Dividend increase: 15% to $0.15 per share quarterly

## 2025 Guidance Update
- Full-year production growth raised to ~10% (vs. original 5-7%)
- Q4 production guidance: 101,000 BOE/day (new record expected)
- Q4 capital spending: $110 million

## Cost Structure
- Adjusted cash operating costs: $11.36 per BOE
- Q4 LOE guidance: $5.20 per BOE
- Price differential: ~$3/barrel discount to Magellan East Houston

## Management Commentary
Stavros emphasized operational flexibility: "We have a lot of both financial and operational flexibility, especially considering some of those deferrals that have snaked through the system in 2025."

The company deferred well completions into 2026, supporting a ~5% reduction in 2025 capital spending while maintaining production strength through enhanced well performance.

## 2026 Outlook
- Two drilling rigs and one completion crew (unchanged)
- Capital spending: 55% of adjusted EBITDAX (~2025 levels)
- Mid-single-digit total production growth expected
- Modest appraisal capital in Giddings and Karnes
- Bolt-on acquisition strategy continues (small, accretive deals)
- $65 million in bolt-on acquisitions completed YTD 2025
- Fully unhedged position maintained on all volumes
- Dividend growth and share repurchases as core priorities

## Q&A Highlights
- Operational Efficiency: Sustained cost reductions through water handling improvements, chemical management, and crew optimization
- M&A Strategy: Stavros noted significant "white space" in South Texas but emphasized discipline: "The answer is more likely no than yes"
- Commodity Price Flexibility: Maintains program flexibility to defer completions or adjust activity if oil prices weaken substantially
- Expected effective tax rate: 21% (full-year 2025)
- Cash taxes: Zero projected for 2025 (new legislation benefit)
- Senior notes: $400 million maturing 2032
