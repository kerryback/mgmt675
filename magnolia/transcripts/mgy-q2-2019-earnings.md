# Magnolia Oil & Gas (MGY) Q2 2019 Earnings Call Transcript
Source: Motley Fool, August 7, 2019

## Key Speakers
- Steve Chazen - Chairman, President, and CEO
- Chris Stavros - EVP and CFO
- Brian Corales - VP, Investor Relations

## Prepared Remarks

### Steve Chazen
First year as a public entity:
- Production grew 30% since inception without adding debt
- Q2 production: 65,000 BOE/day, up 4% sequentially, 54% oil
- Disciplinary approach: spending within 60% of gross cash flow on D&C
- Karnes County assets generate "the highest economic returns in Lower 48"
- Authorized 10 million share repurchase program

### Chris Stavros
- Q2 net income: $31.3 million ($0.12 per diluted share)
- Revenue: $243 million, up 11% sequentially
- Oil realizations: 107% of WTI
- LOE: $4.20 per BOE; total cash operating costs: $7.66 per BOE
- Adjusted EBITDAX: $182 million
- D&C spending: 64% of adjusted EBITDAX

## Q&A Highlights
- Neal Dingmann (SunTrust): Buybacks vs Giddings. Chazen: Market irrationality makes buybacks attractive; Giddings faces right-of-way issues
- Irene Haas (Imperial Capital): Hedging strategy. Chazen: Generally does not hedge, relies on financial strength as insurance
- Jeff Grampp (Northland): Giddings is "upside flex capital," ranging from multiple rigs to zero depending on prices
- Kashy Harrison (Simmons): ~50,000 acres available for Karnes acquisition, roughly double current holdings
