# Magnolia Oil & Gas (MGY) Q4 2019 Earnings Call Transcript
Source: Motley Fool, February 20, 2020

## Key Speakers
- Steve Chazen - Chairman, President, and CEO
- Chris Stavros - EVP and CFO
- Brian Corales - VP, Investor Relations

## Prepared Remarks

### Steve Chazen
Highlighted 2019 accomplishments as Magnolia's first full calendar year as a public company:
- Invested 60% of cash flow into drilling, completion, and production equipment capital
- Grew production volumes 10% in Q4 2019 versus year-ago period
- Completed multiple small property acquisitions, expanding Karnes acreage by 30%
- Repurchased 7 million shares ($79 million), with 6 million being Class B shares
- Ended year with $47 million more cash despite capital deployment

Giddings Field Progress: "Two wells we brought online at Giddings in the later part of the third quarter produced approximately 1,700 barrels a day during their first 30 days online, and they averaged more than 1,500 barrels a day during the first 120 days."

2020 Strategy: Spend approximately 60% of cash flow on drilling and completion capital. Expected production growth of approximately 6,000 BOE per day. Non-operated activity in Karnes anticipated 15-20% higher than 2019.

Karnes vs Giddings: Karnes wells (Eagle Ford/Austin Chalk) feature quick drilling, high oil cut, peak production within 30 days, sharp decline rates, and high IRRs. Giddings wells (Austin Chalk) produce from natural fractures and fracking, feature larger drainage areas, initially high water production, peak around 90 days, lower decline rates, and significantly larger total hydrocarbon recovery.

### Chris Stavros
Q4 2019 Financial Results:
- Net income: $13.6 million ($0.05 per diluted share)
- Full-year net income: $85 million ($0.28 per diluted share)
- Q4 adjusted EBITDAX: $171 million with 42% spent on D&C capital
- Full-year D&C capital: 60% of adjusted EBITDAX
- Q4 production: 68,300 BOE per day, up 10% year-over-year
- Oil production represented 52% of total volumes
- Q4 free cash flow: $93 million
- Repurchased 6 million Class B shares for $69 million
- Year-end cash: $183 million, debt ~$390 million

Reserve Replacement: Proved developed reserve additions of 35 million BOE in 2019, up 13% YoY, replacing 142% of production. F&D cost: ~$15 per BOE.

2020 Guidance: D&C capital ~60% of adjusted EBITDAX, ~10% well cost reduction vs 2019, ~5% production growth, Giddings oil growth >20%, Q1 production ~65,000 BOE/day.

## Q&A Highlights
- Neal Dingmann (SunTrust): Asked about Giddings development confidence. Chazen: Model delivering better results than predicted. Two-well pad ~1,000 bbl/d oil each plus 3-4 MMcf/d gas.
- Jeff Grampp (Northland): Asked about dividend timeline. Chazen: "We'll get to the dividends over time." Priority is acquiring acreage and maintaining flexibility.
- Leo Mariani (KeyBanc): Asked about free cash flow allocation. Chazen: Cannot control Class B repurchase timing (EnerVest). Confident in bolt-on opportunities as prices stabilize.
- Kashy Harrison (Simmons): Asked about Giddings well costs. Current ~$7 million including science costs. Target $5.5-6 million longer term.
