# Magnolia Oil & Gas (MGY) Q3 2021 Earnings Call Transcript
Source: Motley Fool, November 2, 2021

## Key Speakers
- Stephen Chazen - CEO
- Christopher Stavros - CFO
- Brian Corales - VP, Investor Relations

## Prepared Remarks

### Stephen Chazen
Strong Q3 results driven by disciplined capital spending, moderate production growth, high pre-tax margins and low debt:
- Generated $143 million in free cash flow
- Repurchased 5 million shares (~2% of outstanding)
- Cash balance grew nearly 30% to $245 million
- YTD share repurchases: 22.6 million shares (9% of shares outstanding)
- Production volumes grew 4% sequentially to 67.4 Mboe/d
- Giddings drilling efficiencies: four wells per pad with lateral lengths averaging >7,000 feet
- Maintained two operated drilling rigs
- Paid first interim dividend of $0.08 per share

### Christopher Stavros
- Adjusted net income: $158 million ($0.67 per share)
- Adjusted EBITDAX: $221.5 million
- D&C capital: $67 million (30% of EBITDAX)
- Total operating costs: $9.66 per BOE (14% sequential decline)
- Operating margin: $27.66 per BOE (60% of revenue)
- Q4 guidance: 68,000-70,000 BOE/d production
- 2022 plan: Two operated rigs, mid-to-high single digits YoY production growth

## Q&A Highlights
- Neal Dingmann (SunTrust): Efficiency drivers primarily from Giddings improvements
- Leo Mariani (KeyBanc): Well costs stable despite inflation (only $1-2 added). Maintaining quality crews over aggressive cost-cutting
- Umang Choudhary (Goldman Sachs): At sustained higher prices, dividends become primary cash deployment vehicle
- Chazen on share repurchases: Purchased at ~$13 or less, supporting 10% annual dividend growth potential
- Noel Parks (Tuohy Brothers): Chazen dismissed rate sensitivity: "we're not borrowers of money"
